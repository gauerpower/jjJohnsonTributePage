J.J. Johnson

Jazz Trombone Pioneer

A brief timeline of J.J.'s remarkable life:

  • 1924 - Born in Indianapolis, IN, United States.
  • 1933 - Began playing the piano at the age of nine.
  • 1938 - Began playing the trombone, later to become the instrument he was famous for mastering.
  • 1941-1942 - Began professional career, performing with band leaders Clarence Love and Snookum Russell.
  • 1942-1945 - Performed and recorded Benny Carter.
  • 1945-1946 - Toured and recorded with legendary bandleader William "Count" Basie.
  • 1946-1951 - With encouragement from Charlie Parker and Dizzy Gillespie, J.J. becomes the first trombonist to master the bebop style - a landmark accomplishment in trombone techique.
  • 1951-1953 - J.J. takes a day job as a blueprint inspector, but continues an active music career, including several recording sessions with Miles Davis for Blue Note Records.
  • 1954-1960 - Johnson teams up with fellow trombonist Kai Winding to record the famous "Jay & Kai" series of albums. During these years, J.J. also collaborated with other hard bop-era jazz legends including Freddie Hubbard, Nat Adderly, Cedar Walton, Tommy Flanagan, Max Roach, Stan Getz, and more.
  • 1960's-1970's - J.J. focused his career on composition and arranging, including big band music, "Third Stream" pieces incorporating elements of classical music, and Hollywood film scores.
  • 1987 - J.J. returns to performing and recording, including tours of the U.S., Europe, and Japan.
  • 1988-1991 - J.J. takes time off from music to care for his ailing wife, Vivian, after she suffers a stroke. Following her death in 1991, he dedicated the album Vivian to her memory.
  • 1993-1996 - J.J. remarries and returns to performing and recording music, including winning several Grammy awards.
  • 1996 - Retires from performing and lives a quiet life in Indianapolis.
  • 2001 - Johnson commits suicide by gunshot after battling prostate cancer.

"J. J. did for the trombone what Charlie Parker did for the saxophone. And all of us that are playing today wouldn't be playing the way we're playing if it wasn't for what he did."

-Steve Turre
